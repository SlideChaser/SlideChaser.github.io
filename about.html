amatuer web developer and aspiring software engineer

Growing up in the mountains and in the country, I was building computers and working with B.A.S.I.C. when I was twelve.

I started getting interested in more low level languages, and of course ended up looking into C and C++. This proved pretty intimidating to my young self, and I quickly got overwhelmed. My parents tried to help me move forward with it and my grandma, on my moms side, even got me a nice MacBook that I was supposed to learn more about computer with. It just didn't really happen at the time. But during 2020 I was inspired by an idea I had hanging out with my neighbor watching him play some video games. Long story short I decided I needed to get back into programming if I wanted to have any chance of pushing my idea forward.

I started taking C++ for unreal on Udemy, and then just C++ and then C, and when I was still frustrated that nobody was explaining the "How's" and "Why's" I decided to take Assembly as well. This has helped me understand the principles and inner workings of a computer so much more.

When the local company BitWise started offering virtual courses (You know cause COVID and all) I immediately signed up. It wasn't the low level stuff I wanted to learn, but I pride myself in being multifaceted and what harm could it do to learn more.

And I am so glad that I did, I'm typing this out in VS Code now and I am so excited about how much fun web design can be. Albeit html has it's own set of frustrations that have proved entirely different from C and BASIC. But nonetheless, I have discovered a new way to apply my creativity and it is just too much fun!

Stay tuned ;P
